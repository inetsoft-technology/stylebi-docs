[#DrillThroughChart]
== Drill Through a Chart
When a chart axis displays a date field or a cube dimension, the chart automatically provides the user with the ability to drill down to finer levels of resolution of the date or dimension.

To drill down into a date or cube dimension on a chart axis, follow these steps:

. Hover the mouse over the chart axis that you want to display at greater resolution. For axes that represent date or cube dimension data, this will display a {buttonDrillDown} at the left side of the axis.
+
IMPORTANT: The {buttonDrillDown} is only visible when you hover over the axis.
+
image:DrillDown.png[alt="drill down button on chart axis"]

. Press the {buttonDrillDown} to drill down to the next level of the date or dimension.

. Repeat the above steps to drill down to deeper levels of the hierarchy.
+
image:DrillDown2.png[alt="drill down to deeper hierarchy level"]

. To drill up to a higher level of the date or dimension hierarchy, hover the mouse over the chart axis that you want to display at reduced resolution. This displays a {buttonDrillUp} at the left side of the axis.
+
image:DrillDown3.png[alt="drill up button on chart axis"]

. Press the {buttonDrillUp} to drill up to the higher level of the date or dimension hierarchy.
+
[NOTE]
====
When you drill down into a discretely grouped date axis (e.g., date grouping level set to ‚ÄòQuarter of Year‚Äô, ‚ÄòMonth of Year‚Äô, etc.), this creates a facet chart by repeatedly adding the date field as an additional dimension with a finer grouping level.

image:DrillDown4.png[alt="facet chart from drilling discrete date"]

However, when you drill down into  a continuously grouped date axis (e.g.,  date grouping level set to ‚ÄòQuarter‚Äô, ‚ÄòMonth‚Äô, etc.), this changes the grouping level of the existing dimension rather than adding an additional dimension.

image:DrillDown5.png[alt="continuous date drill down result"]

The exception to this rule is the ‚ÄòYear‚Äô level, which behaves as a discrete grouping and generates a facet chart upon drill down (as illustrated in the steps above).

====

To disable the drilling feature for a particular chart, deselect the ‚ÄòEnable Drilling‚Äô option in the *Advanced* tab of the ‚ÄòChart Properties‚Äô panel. To specify a custom drill hierarchy for any set of hierarchical fields, including custom date levels, use the *Hierarchy* tab. See xref:ChartProperties.adoc[] for more details.

[#DrillThroughCrosstab]
== Drill Through a Crosstab
When a Crosstab header displays a date field or a cube dimension, the Crosstab automatically provides the user with the ability to drill down to finer levels of resolution of the date or dimension.

To drill down into a date or cube dimension on a Crosstab header, follow these steps:


. Hover the mouse over the Crosstab. For headers that represent date or cube dimension data, this will display a {buttonDrillDown} at the left side of the header.
+
IMPORTANT: The {buttonDrillDown} is only visible when you hover over the Crosstab.
+
image:DrillDownCross.png[alt="drill down button on crosstab header"]

. Press the {buttonDrillDown} to drill down to the next level of the date or dimension.

. Repeat the above steps to drill down to deeper levels of the hierarchy.
+
image:DrillDownCross2.png[alt="drill down to deeper crosstab hierarchy"]

. To drill up to a higher level of the date or dimension hierarchy, hover the mouse over the crosstab. This displays a {buttonDrillUp} at the left side of expanded headers.
+
image:DrillDownCross3.png[alt="drill up button on crosstab headers"]

. Press the {buttonDrillUp} to drill up to the higher level of the date or dimension hierarchy.

. To fully expand thfe hierarchy for a dimension value, right-click the value and select the appropriate ‚ÄòExpand Hierarchy‚Äô option. To collapse an expanded hierarchy, select ‚ÄòCollapse Hierarchy‚Äô.
+
image:expandHierarchy1.png[alt="right-click to expand hierarchy"]

. To fully expand all the hierarchies for a dimension (column or row header), right-click a value of the dimension and select the appropriate ‚ÄòExpand Field‚Äô option. To collapse an expanded hierarchy, select ‚ÄòCollapse Field‚Äô.
+
image:expandHierarchy2.png[alt="right-click to expand field"]

. To fully expand *_all_*   hierarchies in the entire crosstab, right-click the table title and select ‚ÄòExpand Hierarchy‚Äô. To collapse *_all_* hierarchies in an expanded table, select ‚ÄòCollapse Hierarchy‚Äô.
+
image:expandHierarchy3.png[alt="right-click to expand all hierarchies"]

To disable the drilling feature for a particular Crosstab, deselect the ‚ÄòEnable Drilling‚Äô option in the *Advanced* tab of the ‚ÄòCrosstab Properties‚Äô dialog box. To specify a custom drill hierarchy  for any set of hierarchical fields, including custom date levels, use the *Hierarchy* tab.  See xref:CrosstabProperties.adoc[] for more details.

== Drill  with a Flyover

A _flyover view_ filters the information displayed in selected Dashboard components when the user moves the mouse over (or clicks on) particular regions of a chart, table, or crosstab. This effectively allows a Chart, Table, or Crosstab to behave as a selection element. Flyover is similar to a *data tip* (see xref:AddTipsToChart.adoc[]), except that the filtered components remain in their original positions (rather than following the mouse).

For example, you may want the Table below to display sales only for the particular product(s) that the user selects on the Chart. A flyover view allows the user to hover the pointer over the Chart to filter the table as desired.

image:Flyover Final.png[alt="flyover view filtering table by chart selection"]

The filtered flyover component and the component that triggers the flyover can be based on the same data block or on different data blocks. In the latter case, the flyover component is filtered based on any matching fields in the two data blocks.

TIP: To access the current flyover filter condition in script, use xref:viewsheetscript:CommonDataConditions.adoc[].

.Walkthrough: Flyover View
****
In this example, you will create a chart that displays quantity purchased by company. When the user hovers over (or clicks) a given company on the chart, this will automatically filter an associated Table and Gauge to display data for only that company.

. Create a new Dashboard based on the ‚ÄòSales Explore‚Äô Data Worksheet.
include::ROOT:partial$CreateNewWorksheetNote.adoc[]
+
include::ROOT:partial$SalesExploreWorksheet.adoc[]

.. From the ‚ÄòSales‚Äô data block (top of the *Toolbox* panel), drag the ‚ÄòQuantity Purchased‚Äô field onto the Gauge. This binds the Gauge to the data block.

.. Right-click the Gauge, and select ‚ÄòProperties‚Äô from the context menu. {rightClickOptions} This opens the ‚ÄòGauge Properties‚Äô panel.

.. Under the *General* tab, change the ‚ÄòName‚Äô property to ‚ÄúQuantityGauge‚Äù.

.. Set the following limits: ‚ÄòMaximum‚Äô=200, ‚ÄòMinor Increment‚Äô=10, ‚ÄòMajor Increment‚Äô=25.

.. Under the *Data* tab, select ‚ÄòCount‚Äô from  the ‚ÄòAggregate‚Äô menu. This will display the number of records (orders) for each company.

.. Press btn:[OK] to close the ‚ÄòGauge Properties‚Äô panel.

. Drag a Table component onto the Dashboard. Follow the steps below to configure the Table:

.. In the ‚ÄòSales‚Äô data block (top of the *Toolbox* panel), select the following fields: ‚ÄòCategory‚Äô, ‚ÄòCompany‚Äô, ‚ÄòName‚Äô.

.. Drag the selected fields onto the Table component. This adds the three columns to the Table. Resize the Table as desired.

.. Right-click the Table, and select ‚ÄòProperties‚Äô from the context menu. {rightClickOptions} This opens the ‚ÄòTable Properties‚Äô panel.

.. Under the *General* tab, change the ‚ÄòName‚Äô property to ‚ÄúCompanyTable‚Äù.

.. Under the *Advanced* tab, select ‚ÄòShrink to Fit‚Äô. This automatically sizes the table to the number of records displayed.

.. Press btn:[OK] to close the dialog box.

. Drag a Chart element onto the Dashboard. Press the {buttonEdit} in the Chart mini-toolbar to open the Visualization Recommender. Press the {buttonFullEditor} at the top right of the Recommender to bypass the Recommender and open the Chart Editor.

.. From the ‚ÄòMeasures‚Äô node, drag the ‚ÄòQuantity Purchased‚Äô measure to the ‚ÄòY‚Äô region. This creates a chart that displays quantity purchased for each company.

.. Press the {buttonEditDimension}, and use the ‚ÄòRanking‚Äô option to display only the top 10 companies. Press the {buttonApply}.
+
image:Chart Data Tip Top 10_2.png[alt="ranking option to show top 10 companies"]

.. {finishEditing}

.. Resize the chart as desired.

[#DrillDownFilter]
== Drill Down and Filter
When a Chart or Crosstab displays data with a defined hierarchy in the *Hierarchy* tab (see xref:ChartProperties.adoc[] and xref:CrosstabProperties.adoc[]), you may want to simultaneously drill and filter the data. For example, if the Chart or Crosstab displays sales totals by product category, you may want to drill down into a particular category and view the totals for the individual products in that specific category.

NOTE: This is different from the operations described in xref:#DrillThroughChart[] and xref:#DrillThroughCrosstab[] above, which drill to the next level of hierarchy but *do not* filter.

If you have configured a ‚ÄòCategory‚Äô > ‚ÄòProduct‚Äô hierarchy for a Chart, then you can drill and filter by following the steps below:

. Select the ‚ÄòCategory‚Äô group(s) on the chart that you want to drill and filter.
+
include::viewsheet:partial$selectChartData.adoc[]
+
image:DrillAndFilter1.png[alt="select category groups on chart"]

. Press the {buttonDrillDownFilter} in the Chart toolbar.
+
image:DrillAndFilter2.png[alt="press Drill Down Filter button"]
+
This drills to the ‚ÄòProduct‚Äô level and filters the Chart to display only the data in the selected ‚ÄòCategory‚Äô. A drill icon indicates that a drill is in effect.
+
image:DrillAndFilter3.png[alt="chart drilled and filtered to product level"]
+
IMPORTANT: All other Dashboard components that display this dataset will also be filtered. In this respect, the {buttonDrillDownFilter} behaves like a Filter component (Selection List, etc.).

. To reverse the drill and filter operation, select a group on the Chart, and then press the {buttonDrillUpFilter} in the Chart toolbar.
+
image:DrillAndFilter4.png[alt="press Drill Up Filter to reverse drill"]
